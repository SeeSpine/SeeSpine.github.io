Frequently Asked Questions

If you are still growing:
a) Curves less than 20°: observation via X-rays every 6 months.
b) Curves between 20° to 50°: continued X-ray monitoring and a back brace-- different types of braces are available depending on the individual curve pattern.
c) Curves greater than 50°: surgery is indicated.

If you are finished growing:
a) Curves less than 50°: no treatment.
b) Curves greater than 50°: surgery.

Repeated radiation exposure can lead to health risks such as cancer and other illnesses. Although X-rays are often necessary for various medical reasons, it is important to avoid repetitive X-rays whenever possible.

Scoliosis patients are subject to many X-rays, leading to an increased risk of breast and other cancers. For this reason, SeeSpine delivers a radiation-free alternative to minimize risk in scoliosis monitoring.

In the United States, the average cost of surgery is $150,000 USD. In Canada, the minimum cost to the healthcare system is $60,000 CAD (similar for other public healthcare systems).

Scoliosis patients typically receive C-rays every 6 months to check for curve progression. Since curves worsen during growth and growth spurts can occur rapidly, patients frequently deteriorate quickly between visits.

By tracking patients monthly, SeeSpine detects growth spurts and curve progression to initiate preventative treatments earlier.

SeeSpine employs artificial intelligence to quantify surface topography (what we can see from the outside) to predict the shape of the spine (what we need X-rays to see)!

The purpose of a back brace is to stop your curve from getting worse. If your monthly SeeSpine scans are stable, we know the brace is working! On the other hand, if you are wearing a brace but your curve continues to progress, we know the brace is ineffective!

It is normal for a brace to cause discomfort, especially for the first few weeks of use. More severe pain is typically a sign that the brace does not fit properly. In this case, the brace should be altered or replaced. It is important to note that as your child grows, they may periodically require a new, larger brace.

Scoliosis patients are allowed to experience back pain just like anyone else! Back pain is not a sign that your curve has gotten worse and should not cause you to worry.

For light or modest pain, we suggest:
a) Physical activity, particularly strengthening abdominal muscles (swimming, yoga, sports, etc.)
b) Changing positions frequently
c) Avoiding painful positions and activities

If your back pain is too severe or does not improve after two months of increased physical activity, we encourage you to contact your physician.

The more you wear the brace, the more effective they are!
If you forget to wear your brace or need to remove it for whatever reason, simply resume wearing it as soon as possible. If your brace wear is paused for an extended period without your physician’s approval, we suggest performing a SeeSpine scan immediately.
