Current Problems With 
Scoliosis Management

Our Solution

1

Radiation Exposure

Scoliosis patients are at twice the risk of breast cancer than the general population due to repetitive X-rays. While radiation exposure is often necessary for a variety of medical situations, it is very important to avoid it whenever possible! The average scoliosis patient is followed for 6 years, leading to 24 
X-rays throughout their follow-up.

Radiation-free alternatives such as SeeSpine help alleviate the risk of developing cancers in the future whil​e still allowing for proper scoliosis monitoring.

Learn More!

2

Discontinuous Follow-Up

Currently, the typical scoliosis patient receives X-rays every 6 months. As curves progress continuously with growth and growth spurts can occur very rapidly, healthcare professionals often miss the chance to intervene at the right moment.

SeeSpine's monthly, at-home monitoring identifies curve progression and growth spurts earlier, allowing for quicker and more efficient treatments!
